What to do if you don't have a printer
If you don't have a printer, write down the application reference number, your name and address and send this to us with your documents. We've also sent you an email with the application reference and office address details, so you have the option to print that at another computer later.
2. Send your documents

Remember to include a self-addressed envelope with the correct stamps on, for the return of your documents.

Help with self-addressed envelopes
  1. Add stamps on to an A4-sized envelope - enough to cover posting your documents back:
    • we recommend you go to your local post office who will help you buy the right postage
    • an A4-sized envelope is classified as a 'Large Letter' by Royal Mail
    • the correct amount depends on the size, weight and delivery preferences - returning 1 document will cost approximately £1 to £2
  2. Write your return address on the envelope - we'll use this envelope to send your documents back to you when they've been legalised.
  3. Put this stamped self-addressed envelope together with your documents in a separate envelope addressed to the Legalisation Office. It's ok to fold it if it doesnt fit.
How long it takes and when you'll be charged

It normally takes 2 working days to process your documents, plus courier or postage time. It might take longer during Easter and summer.

We manage payment as follows:

  • your payment card will be charged when we receive your documents
  • if we haven’t received your documents within 28 days, your payment will be cancelled and the money returned to your account - you will need to make another application
  • if we can’t legalise your documents, we’ll refund your payment, but will charge the courier fee to return your documents to you
